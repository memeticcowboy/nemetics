# World-States — Quick Reference

**Category:** Global coordination topology  
**Function:** Emergent outcomes, never goals

---

## The Bifurcation

| World-State | Glyph | Operator Stack | Defining Feature | Essence |
|-------------|-------|----------------|------------------|---------|
| **Co-SPHERE** | 🜛 | Z ∘ Ψ ∘ Q ∘ χ ⊕ Ω | Ω-permeable coordination | World-weaving, ethical resonance, revisable norms |
| **MemeGrid** | ▩ | Z without Ω | Ω-excluded hard minimum | Control lattice, compulsory doing, identity fixation |

---

## The Core Question

> **Does Z remain Ω-permeable or not?**

- **Yes** → 🜛 Co-SPHERE
- **No** → ▩ MemeGrid

Everything else in the stack serves to **feed that decision**.

---

## Co-SPHERE (🜛)

**Role:** Ω-permeable coordination basin  
**Invariant:** Z collapses without sealing Ω  
**Failure Mode:** Premature coherence

**Characteristics:**
- Soft global minimum
- Invitational descent
- Knots aligned, not fused
- Twists prevalent and beneficial
- Lattice breathes (scaffold, not cage)

---

## MemeGrid (▩)

**Role:** Sealed control lattice  
**Invariant:** Meaning no longer moves  
**Failure Mode:** Total (irreversible without rupture)

**Characteristics:**
- Hard global minimum
- Compulsory descent
- Knots fused, permanent
- Twists forced to Knot
- Lattice hardens (cage, not scaffold)

---

## Key Rules

- World-states are **emergent topologies**, never goals
- World-states describe **global Z-behavior**, not local health
- Can have healthy local Habitats within failing global topology
