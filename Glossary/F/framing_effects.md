# Framing Effects

**Presentation of information shapes decision-making.**

"90% survival" vs "10% mortality" — same facts, opposite choices. Air (σ) distinction-making hijacked by frame; Water (ρ) felt-quality shifts with presentation. The frame becomes invisible infrastructure.

**Principle:** "The glass half-full and half-empty are the same water, but different choices."

**Diagnostic:** Would the decision change if the frame were reversed? What frame is not being shown?

**Related:** WYSIATI, Anchoring, Loss Aversion
