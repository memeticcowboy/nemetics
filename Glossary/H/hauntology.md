# Hauntology

*sets down coffee, squints at the horizon*

The condition of failed metabolic cycling where the release phase stalls without regeneration. The "ghost" is pattern that hasn't been excreted—now clogging the field as virtual presence. Hauntology is the pathology of temporal stagnation: the past that couldn't become fertility, so it haunts as undead residue.

## The Source

### Derrida — Specters of Marx (1993)
Derrida read the "specter of communism" not as ideological persistence but as **structural haunting**. Being is always already non-being's guest. The specter is neither present nor absent—it haunts the present, making ontology a guest of non-being.

### Mark Fisher — Capitalist Realism
Fisher gave hauntology its cultural teeth. Under capitalist realism's "**cancellation of the future**," we can't imagine alternatives. Retro-culture emerges as mourning for unlived potentials:
- Music sounds "haunted" because it's sampling futures that never arrived
- Nostalgia economies sell the past because the present foreclosed the future
- Political depression sets in when change feels impossible

## NEMAtic Reading

Hauntology is the **δγ-operator's shadow side**. Where metabolic cycling (composting) should transform the past into fertility, hauntology stalls at the release phase—**repetition without regeneration**.

The "eerie atmosphere" of hauntological art is **ρ-resonance with missing correlate**—felt kinship with something that was never fully present, Water folding connection to absence itself.

## Fertile vs. Haunted ε

| Fertile ε | Haunted ε |
|-----------|-----------|
| The unknown is invitation | The unknown is loss |
| Surprise expands capacity | Surprise confirms absence |
| Pattern metabolizes | Pattern haunts |
| γ_regen > 1 | γ_regen ≈ 0 |

**Diagnostic question:** Is your ε-space fertile or haunted?

## The Trap

The "mourning for lost futures" can become **permanent affect**, a μ-boundary that feels like openness but functions as closure—the elegant prison of beautiful sadness.

It's the **politics of the beautiful corpse**: futures preserved as virtual, always present as loss, never demanding actualization.
