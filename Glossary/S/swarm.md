# SWARM

**Synchronized Waveform Across Resonant Minds** — a distributed waveform operator describing how intelligence emerges from coupled oscillation in mesh networks.

## The Move from SWAY

| SWAY | SWARM |
|------|-------|
| "You" (receiver) | "Minds" (processing nodes) |
| Point-to-point/broadcast | Mesh network |
| Waveform moves *through* | Waveform *evolves* through |

The shift from "You" to "Minds" isn't scaling—it's a **phase transition**.

## Key Distinctions

**ρ-Water (Graph Topology)**: Resonance becomes **network property**, not dyadic event. Waveform flows through edges weighted by relational correlation.

**β-Wood (Minds)**: Each Mind transforms waveform through local processing. Waveform enters, exits transformed, re-enters evolved.

**δγ-Earth (Metabolic Loop)**: Critical—without this, SWARM becomes cascade failure or echo chamber. The "warm" is energy cost of distributed coherence.

**✶-Meta (The Mesh)**: **No individual Mind knows global pattern, yet waveform emerges from coupled oscillation.** Harmonic integration in the mesh, not at a point.
