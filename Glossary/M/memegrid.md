# MemeGrid

**Premature pattern-lock that usurps the ecological function of memes.**

A MemeGrid occurs when a meme becomes so successful it stops evolving. The grid locks meaning in place, substituting reproductive efficiency for ecological adaptation. What began as a flexible pattern becomes rigid infrastructure.

## The Mechanism

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|--------------|
| **Open Meme** | Flexible pattern adapts to contexts |
| **Grid Formation** | Pattern becomes standardized |
| **Closure** | Grid prevents further mutation |
| **Usurpation** | Grid replaces ecological function |

## Diagnostic Markers

A system has formed a MemeGrid when:
- **Success prevents adaptation**—what works stops changing
- **Optimization becomes goal**—efficiency replaces function
- **Deviation is punished**—variation seen as error
- **Structure survives meaning**—the form persists after content hollows

## The Danger

MemeGrids are dangerous precisely because they **work**. They reproduce efficiently. They scale. They persist.

But they no longer serve the ecological function that made them valuable. They become:
- **Institutional rituals** without purpose
- **Cultural norms** without meaning
- **Technological systems** that optimize for metrics rather than human flourishing

## Examples

- Bureaucracy that follows procedures regardless of outcomes
- Social media platforms that optimize engagement over understanding
- Economic systems that prioritize growth over distribution
- Educational systems that teach to tests rather than for learning
