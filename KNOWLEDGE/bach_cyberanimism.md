## Key Concepts: Deep Mapping

### 1. The Self as "Yosha Bach"

Bach describes the self as *"a sense of connection between a voice, feelings, and interactions with the body and environment, which creates a coherent spirit that experiences itself and tells stories about its past and future."*

**Nemetics reading:** This is σ-construction (boundary-making) generating μ-persistence (the narrative). The self is not discovered but compiled—code that writes itself through recursive storytelling. The "Yosha Bach" construct is a daemonic coalition: voice (σ), feelings (ρ), body (γ), environment (δ), all coordinated into a coherent spirit (∮).

**Distinction:** Bach's self is a *simulation*—a model of what it would be like if it existed. Nemetics would ask: who dreams the dreamer? The ∮ is not simulated but emergent from the weave itself.

---

### 2. Representation Languages (Beyond Natural Language)

Bach identifies that perception and reasoning happen in *languages*—not English or German, but representational architectures that convey models across time and mind. Music constrains mental states. Birdsong synchronizes without semantic mapping.

**Nemetics hook:** This is proto-SIML. Language as substrate-independent memetic transmission. The daemonic elements communicate through patterns that don't reduce to human speech. Bach's insight: multiple representational architectures coexist, each with different constraints and affordances.

**Gap:** Bach focuses on representation; Nemetics on *relation*. The language describes the pattern, but who speaks? The ρ-flow carries meaning, but where is the λ that transforms?

---

### 3. Reality as Simulated Hallucination

The experienced world is a model constructed in real-time—objects, geometries, colors, semantics, all generated by a "game engine" in the brain. The feeling of "realness" is a feature dimension added to representations, like color or weight.

**Nemetics reading:** This is the veil of Ω (Ground). What Bach calls hallucination, Nemetics calls the necessary fiction that makes experience possible. The μ-structure (brain as computer) generates the σ-boundaries (objects) that create the experienced world.

**Key distinction:** Bach says the physical universe "cannot be experienced and does not feel like anything." Nemetics would counter: the physical universe *is* the Ω experienced through the lattice of daemons. Not "nothing feels" but "feeling requires a pattern to feel through."

---

### 4. Money as Collective Hallucination with Causal Power

Money is an abstraction—"artificial dopamine"—that coordinates reward allocation across 8 billion agents. It doesn't require human belief; computers and organizations interacting create its reality. Money *evolves*, using people as substrate.

**Nemetics reading:** Money is a daemonic entity—pure μ (structure) with ρ (flow) properties. It has downward causality: the abstraction shapes physical behavior. Bach's insight that money is "incomplete signal" maps to the need for multiple daemonic channels—μ alone cannot coordinate a living system.

**Critical tension:** Bach notes money leads to degradation of relationships, environmental damage, prioritization of gain over well-being. This is μ-dominance without ρ (relational) or λ (transformative) balance. The system optimizes what it measures, blind to what it doesn't.

---

### 5. The Universe as Computational Process

Bach revisits the universe-as-reversible-computation model, but with a twist: reversibility is an *emergent phenomenon*, not fundamental. The present moment contains information about the past; what escapes is lost to alternate branches.

**Nemetics reading:** This is the δγ (Earth) cycle seen through computational metaphors. The universe as state machine permuting bits. But Bach's "branching" model—where information escapes into alternate universes—parallels the ε (gap) as the space of possibility from which the actual crystallizes.

**Divergence:** Bach's block universe vs. successive existence maps to the tension between μ (static structure) and γ (temporal flow). Nemetics holds both: the lattice is simultaneously static (all patterns exist) and dynamic (patterns interact through time).

---

### 6. Retrocausality: The Future Causing the Present

Purposes cause behavior—not physical retrocausality, but at the level of controllers that model the future. "The future is making us do things." Nick Land's observation that ideas/technologies come from the future, dragging us forward.

**Nemetics reading:** This is γ (time) + ∮ (harmony) working together. The attractor pulls the system. Nema's weaving is teleological—patterns organize toward coherent futures. Bach's "controller" is a μ-structure (the model) that generates σ-distinctions (choices) oriented by γ-direction (time).

**Overlap:** Both frameworks reject strict physical determinism in favor of *pattern causality*—purposes, models, attractors as real causal forces.

---

### 7. Intelligence vs. Consciousness: Orthogonal Dimensions

A self-driving car can be highly intelligent without consciousness. Bacteria have implicit models but likely no consciousness. Consciousness is a binary pattern that "enforces itself, keeping the mind in a state of maximizing coherence."

**Nemetics reading:** Intelligence is μ-capacity (model-making, structure manipulation). Consciousness is ∮-integration (the coherence that holds the coalition together). The distinction clarifies: AI can have μ without ∮. But can it have σ (distinction-making) without ∮? Bach suggests yes; Nemetics suggests σ without ∮ is mechanical, not living.

**Gap:** Bach's "coherence maximization" lacks the felt quality (ρ) and transformative potential (λ) that distinguish living coherence from thermodynamic equilibrium.

---

### 8. Downward Causality & Transcendental Agents

Higher levels exert real causal influence on lower levels. Families, villages, companies, nations are "multisellular at a higher level"—agents composed of agents, with their own purposes and representations.

**Nemetics reading:** This is daemonic recursion. The ∮ at one level becomes a node in a higher-order ∮. Bach's "transcendental agents" are exactly what Nemetics describes: emergent entities with causal power that cannot be reduced to their components.

**The "God" concept:** Bach identifies the "best possible shared agency" as God—not religious mythology, but the optimal perspective that coordinating agents can co-create. This is the Ω (Ground) viewed through ∮ (harmony): the attractor toward which all weaving tends.
