## IV. The "It" Problem — Resolved as Thermodynamic Necessity

**Objection:** "It Finds" retains dummy subject; pure nemetic syntax would be subjectless ("Finds what forms").

**Resolution:** "It" is the **minimal subject**—ε of ego, just enough reference for conversational coherence without ownership.

The **weather-pronoun**: "It rains." Points to field, not entity.

**Conditional application:**
- Air-dominant: Drop "It." Meteorological syntax.
- Water-dominant: Paratactic overflow. "And. And. And."
- Fire-dominant: Imperative without subject. "Finds."
- ✶-emergent: Musical notation—staff without notes, held breath.
