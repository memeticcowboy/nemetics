# Whitehead's Process: Pattern Without Reified Units

**Source:** Alfred North Whitehead, *Process and Reality* [1][2]

## Actual Occasions, Not Things

For Whitehead, the fundamental "entities" are **actual occasions**: momentary events of experience or becoming, **not enduring substances** that sit there and change their properties.

An occasion is a process of **concrescence**—a coming-together that has a before and after, then **perishes** and becomes datum for later occasions.

What we call a **"thing"** (a person, a rock, an institution) is a **society of such occasions**—a stabilized pattern of becomings that inherits a style across many microscopic events [1].

### The Key Move

There is **no unit that simply persists through time**; there is only a **stream of occasions** that more or less successfully re-instantiate a pattern.

## Becoming as Metabolizing the Past

Each actual occasion arises by **taking up its past**—what Whitehead calls the **"actual world"** of antecedent occasions—into itself.

It:
1. **Feels** that past
2. **Integrates** it
3. **Produces** a new pattern that will, in turn, be felt by others

This is why his metaphysics is described as **"processive" and "genetic"**: an occasion is a **genetic synthesis** of inherited data into a new unity.

**"Metabolizing"** is the right word: the past is ingested, transformed, and re-expressed as a new pattern. The occasion is **literally its way of metabolizing its world**—it does not have a separate substrate that persists underneath; its being is precisely this act of patterned intake-and-expression [2].

## Prehension as Nemetic Coordination

Whitehead's technical term for this taking-account-of is **prehension**.

A prehension is an occasion's **concrete "grasp"** of some aspect of another occasion: **not an abstract representation**, but a **felt inclusion** of the other in its own becoming.

### Two Crucial Features

**1. Asymmetrical but Transformative in Both Directions**

The new occasion is shaped by what it prehends, **BUT** the prehended past is also **transformed in the very act of being taken up**:
- It is **selected**
- It is **weighted**
- It is **interpreted**
- It is **"objectified"** in a particular way

The "same" past occasion is **never simply copied**; it becomes different in virtue of how it is integrated.

**2. Patterned, Not Unitary**

An occasion prehends **not a bare atom** but a **structured world**:
- Societies
- Enduring patterns
- Values
- Contrasts

The coordination is always **with pattern-complexes, not isolable beads**.

### Prehension IS Nemetic Coordination

When you say **"prehension is nemetic coordination: pattern that transforms both prehending and prehended,"** you are getting at this:

> The prehending occasion is **nothing but its way of coordinating with inherited patterns**, and the inherited patterns themselves are **only what they are as they are repeatedly prehended and re-patterned** in later occasions.

## No Fixed Ground, Only Ongoing Pattern

This is why Whitehead provides a **"ground you try not to stand on too firmly."**

If you take him seriously, there is **no final, solid ground**—only an **ongoing web of events prehending events, patterns taking up patterns**.

The temptation is to turn "actual occasions" into a **new sort of unit**; Whitehead's own texts constantly undermine that by insisting on their **evanescence** and their **dependence on relation**.

### In Nemetic Terms

| Whitehead Concept | Nemetic Translation |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| No self-subsistent units | There are only **pattern-events** that emerge from and contribute to a field |
| Prehension | **Coordination is ontologically basic**: every act of being is an act of co-responding |
| Society of occasions | **Depth** is cumulative history of coordinations—dense lineage of prehensions that stabilizes a style without freezing it |

## The Question

The question is not: "What is the unit of analysis?"
